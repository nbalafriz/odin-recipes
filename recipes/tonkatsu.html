Tonkatsu

Here’s a classic and crispy Tonkatsu (Japanese Pork Cutlet) recipe that’s easy to master and restaurant-worthy. Perfect for pairing with shredded cabbage, rice, and a drizzle of tonkatsu sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless pork loin chops (about ½ inch thick)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1½ cups panko breadcrumbs
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Shredded cabbage (for serving)
  • Tonkatsu sauce (store-bought or homemade)

Optional Homemade Sauce:

  • 2 tbsp ketchup
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 2 tsp sugar

Instructions:

  1. Prep the pork: Trim excess fat and make small slits along the edge to prevent curling. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Dredge: Coat each pork chop in flour, dip in beaten egg, then press into panko until fully coated.
  3. Fry: Heat oil to 170–180°C (340–355°F). Fry pork until golden brown and crispy, about 1½–2 minutes per side. Drain on paper towels.
  4. Serve: Slice into strips and plate with shredded cabbage and tonkatsu sauce.
  5. Drizzle over pork and serve with steamed rice or mantou buns.
