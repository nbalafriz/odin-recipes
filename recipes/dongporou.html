Dong Po Rou (Braised Pork Belly)

Here’s a classic recipe for Dong Po Rou (东坡肉) — a rich, melt-in-your-mouth Chinese braised pork belly dish named after the Song dynasty poet Su Dongpo:

Ingredients (serves 6–8):

  • 2 lbs pork belly (cut into 2–3 inch cubes)
  • 3 bunches scallions (layered at bottom of pot)
  • 8 slices ginger
  • 2 cups Shaoxing wine
  • ⅔ cup light soy sauce
  • 2½ tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 5 oz rock sugar

Instructions:

  1. Prep the pork belly
  2. Blanch the whole slab in boiling water for 1 minute to remove impurities.
  3. Drain and cut into uniform cubes (2–3 inches).
  4. Layer aromatics
  5. In a clay pot or heavy-bottomed pot, lay scallions evenly across the bottom.
  6. Place ginger slices on top of scallions.
  7. Add pork and seasonings
  8. Arrange pork belly skin-side down over aromatics.
  9. Pour in Shaoxing wine, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and add rock sugar.
  10. Simmer gently
  11. Cover and bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  12. Once boiling, reduce to low and simmer for 90 minutes without stirring.
  13. Finish cooking
  14. Flip pork skin-side up.
  15. Continue simmering for another 90 minutes.
  16. Alternatively, transfer to a steamer for the final 90 minutes for a restaurant-style finish.
  17. Serve

Optional:

  • Optionally reduce the braising liquid into a glossy sauce.
  • Drizzle over pork and serve with steamed rice or mantou buns.
