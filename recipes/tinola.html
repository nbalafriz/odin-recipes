Tinolang Manok

What is Tinolang Manok

Tinolang manok traditionally consists of chicken pieces cooked in broth flavored with ginger, onion and fish sauce. Green papaya and pepper leaves are then added afterwards.

INGREDIENTS

  • Chicken pieces – I use chicken thighs in this recipe but other pieces of chicken may also be used.
  • Aromatics: ginger and onion – adds aroma and impart deep flavor to this dish.
  • Vegetable oil – brings out the flavor of the aromatics.
  • Fish sauce or salt – adds a savory and umami flavor to this dish
  • Water – is used to cook and soften the chicken pieces
  • Papaya – add sweetness and texture to the broth
  • Spinach leaves – add flavor and texture as well as nutritional benefits
  • Optional: serrano pepper – adds a certain taste and flavor to the broth

STEPS

  1. To make this chicken tinola (tinolang manok) recipe, first season the chicken pieces with salt.
  2. Next, sauté the chicken with the aromatics until brown and the meat is no longer pink.
  3. Then, season the browned chicken with fish sauce.
  4. Add the water next, and bring the mixture to a boil.
  5. Lower the heat and cook the chicken for 35 minutes.
  6. Next, add the papaya and further cook for 5 minutes.
  7. Correct the seasoning by adding more fish sauce or salt, if needed.
  8. Finally add the leaves (spinach or pepper) and serrano pepper, if using.
  9. Turn off the heat, cover the pot and let stand for a few minutes to let the spinach and serrano peppers impart their flavors into the broth.

TIPS

  • To achieve tastier and flavored chicken pieces, season them before cooking.
  • Make sure to give the chicken pieces time to brown before adding the water. This step is necessary for flavorful chicken pieces.
  • Other regions use chayote instead of papaya.
